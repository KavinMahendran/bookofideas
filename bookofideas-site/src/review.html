What our reader's say about us

reviews from the readers of the book

Ben Dobson

As a freelance motion-designer/animator who perpetually struggles with imposter syndrome and deadline-anxieties, both books in this series served as a really good anchor to help keep me focussed. Particularly through my poorly timed (Covid...) return to freelance life. They’re both chock-full of essential survival tips from Radim Malinic who has himself been ‘through the mill’ a little in his own career, and thus is well placed to share his kindness/wisdom on this front.

Freelancer

CN Rooney

The Book of Ideas is part portfolio, part journal of one designer's experiences and lessons in the industry. As someone who lost their interest in design because of 'cheap and quick' outputs, this book rekindled my enthusiasm for design, in particular book design. This book is truly beautiful, and serves as a potent reminder that design is everywhere. It's sometimes hard to remember that because we have so much competing for our attention. This book makes you stop. I'm grateful for the lessons shared.
